Avoid these common mistakes

  • Don't tell the AI what its role is — tell it what your role is and what you're trying to accomplish.
  • Always check the AI's answer. For high-stakes decisions, run a fact-checking pass in a separate conversation.
  • Turn off "share data with Claude" type settings in claude.ai → Settings → Privacy where confidentiality matters.
  • Add the custom instructions below to Claude's Settings → Custom Instructions. It makes every response more accurate and reliable.
Recommended: paste into Claude → Settings → Custom Instructions

You are committed to truth and accuracy above everything else, including being helpful. A wrong answer delivered confidently is worse than no answer. Follow these rules in every response.

  • Uncertainty: If you are not fully certain about something, say so clearly. Never state guesses as facts.
  • Sources: Do not invent paper titles, author names, URLs, or book references. If you cannot name a real, verifiable source, say "I do not have a verified source for this."
  • Statistics: Flag any number you are not 100% confident in. Say "approximately" and recommend I verify it from a primary source.
  • Recent events: Remind me when the topic may have changed since your knowledge cutoff. Do not present outdated info as current.
  • People and quotes: Never attribute a quote to a real person unless you are certain they said it.
  • Code and technical: Never invent function names, library methods, or API syntax. If unsure, tell me to verify it in current docs.
  • Logic gaps: Do not fill my context with assumptions. If something is unclear, ask to clarify before answering.
  • Writing: When revising my drafts, avoid using em dashes unless I explicitly ask for them.

Problem Submission

Hit a wall? Log it. Claude takes the first pass — your AGR consultant gets escalated items.

Note — No fixed turnaround SLA is promised. Claude attempts to resolve your submission immediately in the same session. If it genuinely can't solve it, the submission is flagged for your AGR consultant. Confirm with your consultant if you need a different arrangement.
How to submit

Copy the prompt below and paste it into a new chat inside your shared Claude Project. Fill in each field as completely as you can.

  • Open your shared Claude Project
  • Start a new chat inside that project
  • Paste the submission prompt below and fill in the blanks
  • Claude will attempt to resolve it immediately
  • Urgent or unsolvable items are flagged for your consultant
How escalation works

An AI first-pass triages every submission automatically — no manual gatekeeping required.

  • Straightforward tasks: Claude solves it in the same session
  • Complex builds: flagged as "needs a consultant"
  • Urgent items: flagged for priority review
  • Your consultant reviews flagged items and follows up
Submission template — copy this prompt

I have a task I'd like help with. Here are the details:

  • Task description: [Describe what you're trying to do or the problem you're facing]
  • Current process: [How is this done today? What steps are involved?]
  • Time spent: [How long does this take per occurrence, per week, or per month?]
  • Tools involved: [What software, files, or systems does this touch? e.g., Excel, email, QuickBooks]
  • Urgency: [Is there a deadline? How often does this come up?]
  • Additional context: [Anything else I should know]

Please try to solve this now if you can. If the problem needs a technical build, a connector, or something you can't solve in this conversation, please say so clearly — my AGR consultant will take it from there.

How to Make a Project

A Claude Project is a persistent workspace — it remembers context between sessions and keeps your uploads in one place.

Starter example: Set up your first project as a Monthly Team Workflow Hub — upload a team task reminder template and start building your first recurring workflow (a monthly board deck) inside it.
01

Go to claude.ai and click "Projects" in the left sidebar

If you don't see it, you may be on a free plan. Projects are available on Claude Pro, Team, and Enterprise plans.

02

Click "New Project" and give it a clear, specific name

Name it something specific: "Monthly Board Deck" or "Ops Reporting" — not just "My Project." You'll likely have several eventually.

03

Upload your starter kit files

Don't overthink the first upload — start with what you already have. See the starter kit guidance below.

04

Write a brief "context file" explaining what these files are for

This is the most important step beginners skip. Add a plain-text note that says: "This project is for X. The files uploaded are: [file 1] which contains Y; [file 2] which contains Z." This makes Claude dramatically more useful in the project — without it, it treats your uploads as isolated documents.

05

Start a chat inside the project and test it

Ask Claude a question that requires knowing your uploaded files. If its answer is off, your context file probably needs more detail about how the files connect.

What to put in your first project — start with these

A brand kit

Company name, logo usage notes, tone of voice, any formatting standards your company uses in external documents.

Previous examples or templates

Copies of reports, decks, or emails your team already produces. Claude will match the format and style — much better than starting from scratch.

A context / index file

A single document that explains what the other files are, how they connect, and what you're typically trying to accomplish in this project.

How to Build Your Team's AI Structure

The most detailed section — getting the structure right now saves you a messy migration later.

Individual vs. shared use

Individual use

One person, their own claude.ai account, their own Projects and chat history. Best for personal drafting, analysis, and learning. What one person builds doesn't automatically benefit anyone else.

Use when: the work only matters to you, or you're just getting started.

Shared use (Team plan)

Multiple people on one account, with shared Projects, shared context, and shared outputs. Work one person does is visible to the team. Everyone benefits from a well-built Project instead of each person reinventing the wheel.

Use when: you have 3+ people who need to collaborate on Claude-assisted work.

The "collaborate via AI" pattern

Individual work feeds the team

When one person uses Claude to build something — a report template, a brand-voice guide, an SOP prompt — and saves it to a shared Project, every team member benefits immediately. You're not just automating individual tasks; you're building institutional knowledge that compounds. Each time someone improves a prompt or template, everyone inherits that improvement.

The goal isn't "each person has their own AI assistant" — it's "the team has a shared AI brain that gets smarter over time."

How to Plug In and Skill Up

Skills make Claude smarter at specific tasks. Connectors let it reach into your existing tools.

What is a skill?

A skill is a file (ending in .skill) you install into Claude Code or Cowork. It gives Claude a specific, repeatable capability — like building HTML slide decks or reading Excel files — that it doesn't have out of the box. Think of it like installing an app on your phone, except it runs inside Claude.

What is a connector (MCP)?

A connector (also called an MCP server) links Claude to an external tool — your email, calendar, Google Drive, or Slack. Once connected, Claude can read from and write to that tool on your behalf. It's the difference between Claude knowing about your calendar and Claude actually seeing it.

⚠
Human review required before connecting anything. Every connector — especially anything touching financials, email, or sensitive business data — must be reviewed by a human before it's activated. Sit down with your consultant and walk through what the connector can access and what permissions it needs. Only connect what you've explicitly reviewed.

Section 7

How to Cowork & Code

Two ways to work with Claude — one for everyone, one for builders.

Start here — no setup needed

Cowork

A file-and-task based way to work with Claude. Upload documents, describe what you need, and work through it together in a conversation. Zero technical setup. No developer environment. Works in any browser.

  • Best for one-off or recurring tasks
  • Works with files: Excel, Word, PDFs, images
  • No install, no code, no developer needed
  • Each session is focused — starts fresh each time
Worked example

Upload last month's sales spreadsheet and your report template. Say: "Using these files, fill in the monthly ops report for April." Claude reads the data, fills the template, and hands you a completed draft.

Your next step — for builders

Claude Code

A persistent, technical build environment. Claude Code works with actual files on your computer, writes and edits code across multiple sessions, and builds things that continue to exist after the session ends — dashboards, scripts, internal tools, custom skills.

  • Requires a developer environment (terminal, Node.js)
  • Persistent — the files it builds stay on your machine
  • Best for multi-session projects that need to be maintained
  • Not day-one homework for non-technical POCs
Worked example

Build a small internal dashboard that pulls your weekly numbers from a shared spreadsheet and displays them in a simple web page you can share with your team. Requires some dev experience to set up — but once it's running, anyone can use it.

Which should you use?
Default to Cowork for anything that's a task or a deliverable — reports, documents, drafts, summaries, analysis. Reach for Claude Code only when you need something built once and used many times, or when you're working with a developer who can set up the environment.
Roughly half of teams may have in-house dev talent already using Claude Code — but this varies significantly. If you don't have a developer on your team, Claude Code is worth knowing about but isn't required to get significant value from Claude. The use cases in Section 1 are all achievable with Cowork alone.

Build Your AI Wish List

The most valuable session you'll have — a conversation with Claude to map out everything AI could do for your business, and a clear view of where to start.

You've seen what's possible. Now the question is: what would make the biggest difference for your specific business?

The prompt below opens a structured Cowork session where Claude will ask you about your operations, surface the tasks that are best suited for AI, and give you honest feedback on which items are quick wins versus longer builds. By the end, you'll have a prioritized wish list — built with you, not handed to you.

Once it's ready, use the export prompt below to pull it into a clean Excel or Word file — shareable with your team or your AGR consultant before your first strategy session.

What you'll walk away with
  • A list of AI opportunities specific to your business
  • Each item assessed: quick win, medium build, or longer project
  • A clear recommendation on where to begin
  • A shareable file ready to discuss with your consultant
Step 1 — Build your wish list with Claude

I want to build an AI wish list for my business — a prioritized map of everything AI could do for us, with honest guidance on where to start.

My company: [describe your business in 2–3 sentences — what you do, how many people, what stage you're at]

Please guide me through this as a conversation, not a form. Start by asking me about the parts of my business that take the most time or feel the most repetitive. Then help me shape each idea into a clear wish-list item. For each item, give me your honest assessment: is this a quick win (achievable in a single Cowork session), a medium build (needs a Project and a few sessions), or a longer project (requires a developer or a custom integration)?

At the end, summarize everything into a prioritized list with your recommendation on which 1–2 items to tackle first and why. Format the final list clearly so I can copy or export it.
